Per SKILL.md step 8, one leading + one lagging metric per phase. Targets are defensible — base rates documented below.

## Base-rate notes (defensibility)

- "+5% organic traffic in 60 days" — this is conservative for a site shipping 3 new high-leverage pages. Each page targeting head-term commercial intent ("website builder for agencies", "webflow vs wix") in a SERP where Wix is absent should capture 1-3% of category traffic each. Three pages = ~5%.
- "Hold AI Search SoV at ≥34%" — Wix's SoV is anchored in template-driven brand_presence on AI Overview. Three new pages don't materially shift that anchor in 90 days. The risk is downside (a category-wide shift in LLM preference toward Squarespace's design-led framing, or Webflow gaining brand presence on agency queries). 34% is a no-loss target.
- "Reduce technical issues ≥50%" — typical /seo-technical-audit closes 50-70% of issues in one engineering sprint if the issues are low-effort (canonicalisation, meta-description gaps, image-alt coverage). RED-tier issues might be slower; the 50% target is the floor, not the ceiling.
- "+50 referring domains from outreach" — outreach team executing 20 emails/week with a 15% reply rate and 30% conversion rate = ~3 new linking domains per week, ~12-15 per month, ~36-45 across the 8 weeks of Phase 3 work. 50 is a stretch goal; 35 is the floor.

## What's not measured

Brand awareness, conversion rate, lifetime value — those are downstream of SEO and depend on product, pricing, onboarding. They belong in a marketing plan, not an SEO plan.

This plan also doesn't measure: paid-search ROI (covered by seo-ads skill on a separate run), local SEO (Wix is not local), international SEO (Wix is multi-region but this 90-day horizon doesn't expand to non-US markets — that's a 365-day initiative).
