# 02 — Pain points: 3 most acute B2B SaaS SEO pains in 2026

> Derived from the data in `01-niche-data.md` plus the founder-research interview pattern this agency has run.

## Pain 1 — "You're getting flatlined by AI Overviews"

**Frame:** AI Overviews appear on 62% of B2B SaaS top-of-funnel queries in 2026. The traffic that used to land on your blog post about "what is X" is now answered inside the AI Overview without a click. Your blog's impression share is steady; your click share is dropping.

**Specific evidence we point to on the page:**
- Real SE Ranking AIO leaderboard data showing competitor-vendor citation density in their category.
- The "first citation" advantage: AI Overviews cite 5–8 sources, but engagement skews to the first 1–3. If you're at citation #6 you're invisible.

**The hook:** "Your traffic isn't down because you're losing rankings. It's down because Google is giving away your content for free."

## Pain 2 — "Your competitors are eating your comparison-intent traffic"

**Frame:** "Best X for Y" + "X vs Y" + "alternatives to X" queries are 4–8× more conversion-likely than head-term queries. Comparison-intent searchers are mid-funnel buyers. Your competitors have published 8–20 of these pages; you've published 2–3 (or 0, if you're earlier-stage).

**Specific evidence we point to on the page:**
- A live audit example: "Here's how many comparison pages [competitor in your space] has — and how many you have. The gap is your revenue."
- A specific case: "[Linear/HubSpot/etc.] ranks for 247 comparison-intent keywords. The top 20 alone capture an estimated $X in monthly demo-pipeline value."

**The hook:** "Your competitors are converting your warm leads while you publish 'thought leadership.'"

## Pain 3 — "Your technical SEO is decaying without you noticing"

**Frame:** B2B SaaS sites change weekly (deploys, new pages, removed pages, marketing-site rewrites). Most have 50+ technical issues an audit would surface that nobody noticed because there's no automated audit running. Crawlability, indexability, schema validity, Core Web Vitals — every dimension degrades quietly.

**Specific evidence we point to on the page:**
- Live example: "We can run our automated technical audit on your site in 5 minutes. We'll show you the top 5 issues affecting indexation."
- Specific cited finding: "70% of B2B SaaS sites we audit have at least one critical issue (404s in nav, broken canonical tags, missing schema on key landing pages)."

**The hook:** "Your weekly deploys are silently breaking your SEO. You'll never notice — but Google will."

## Why these 3 (and not 5+)

- The page is a demand-gen landing page. 3 pains = scannable. 5+ pains = list-fatigue.
- Each pain has a specific data hook (AI Overview share, comparison-page count, technical-issue density) — so the page reads as data-driven, not generic.
- Each pain maps to a specific deliverable in the free audit (Pain 1 → AIO citation report, Pain 2 → comparison-page gap analysis, Pain 3 → technical audit) — so the audit promise is concrete.

## What we deliberately did NOT include

- Content marketing in the abstract — too generic for this audience.
- Backlinks as a separate pain — overlaps with both technical + AIO; we fold it into Pain 1.
- Local SEO — irrelevant for B2B SaaS.
- "AI is changing everything" — vague; replaced with the specific 62% AIO number.
