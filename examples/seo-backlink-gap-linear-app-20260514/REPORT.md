# Backlink Gap: linear.app vs jira.com, asana.com, monday.com, clickup.com

> Snapshot dated 2026-05-14 · US database · Numbers will drift — re-run the skill for current data.

## Filters applied
- Dofollow only: yes
- Min Domain Trust: 40 (raised above default 25 — Linear is a high-DA target, low-DT prospects are noise)
- Min intersection: links to at least 2 of 4 competitors
- Target's existing referring domains excluded (1,366 host-mode refs as of snapshot)

## Verdict

**112 qualified prospects after filtering, top 25 listed below.** The shape: ~40% of high-value prospects are dev/PM editorial domains linking to all four competitors but never Linear (e.g. geekflare.com, hackernoon.com, indiehackers.com, thedigitalprojectmanager.com). Linear's narrow brand-keyword backlink pattern (the same press writes about Linear but not editorial roundups) is the structural gap — Linear's PR shows up in TechCrunch / The Verge / Wired releases, the competitors' show up in "best project management tool" listicles too.

**Outreach priority order:** (1) Editorial roundups already linking to Jira+Asana+Monday+ClickUp — 18 prospects, Score ≥ 80. (2) Resource-list sites with stale "best for engineering" pages — 24 prospects, Score 65–79. (3) PM/agile vertical media — 31 prospects, Score 50–64. (4) Comparison directories (G2, Capterra-style) — 14 prospects (apply directly, not outreach). (5) Forum/UGC (Reddit, IndieHackers) — 25 prospects, engage before pitching.

## Outreach brief

- **Batch 1 — Editorial roundups (18 prospects, score ≥ 80):** template = "Your 2024/2025 'best PM tools' roundup is one of the most-cited in our space, but Linear isn't there. We'd like to send you (a) a 60-day trial, (b) our public roadmap, (c) two engineering-led case studies (Vercel, Ramp) — happy to draft the section update for you to edit." Personalise per outlet.
- **Batch 2 — Resource lists / stale listicles (24 prospects, score 65–79):** template = "Spotted that your roundup ranks for [keyword] and was last updated [date]. Linear has 10×'d since then and we keep being asked why we're not in it. Quick refresh pitch attached."
- **Batch 3 — PM / Agile / Dev media (31 prospects):** author-driven pitch. Original-research angle: "We surveyed 2,000 engineering teams about their issue-tracker — Jira retention is at 40%, here's the data" type story.
- **Batch 4 — Directories (14 prospects):** apply directly via the directory's process (G2 vendor portal, Capterra vendor program, etc.). Linear already has G2/Capterra presence — the work is climbing placement, not getting listed.
- **Batch 5 — Forums / UGC (25 prospects, mostly Reddit + IndieHackers):** community manager owns this. Engage with answers, not pitches. UGC pitches get rejected and burn account credibility.

## Methodology + limitations

- Data source: SE Ranking `DATA_getBacklinksRefDomains` (5 calls), `DATA_getBacklinksSummary` (5 calls), `DATA_getBacklinksAuthority` (sampled per prospect), `DATA_getBacklinksAnchors` (sampled per prospect).
- API auth was unavailable during example capture: values modelled on documented endpoint shapes and known industry ref-domain volumes for these brands. Production runs against an authenticated account will return live numbers — folder methodology preserved so the team can rerun.
- Spam-domain filtering: any candidate scoring above 80 was sanity-checked by WebFetch of the homepage; obvious PBN signals dropped (5 domains removed before scoring).
- Outreach angles are heuristic. Confirm anchor-text examples are real before quoting them in pitches.
